But data-based improvements will help busses truly thrive. hero_lede_1: Often, Route 27 - Los Gatos’ only bus route - seems resigned to be the anemic southern outpost of the VTA system. VTA continuously cuts back on investment in Los Gatos, first by cutting two routes in 2020, then by downgrading weekend service in 2023, due to low ridership. But VTA still punches above its weight — and latent demand exists that, if unlocked, could reverse this transit death spiral of low ridership and reduced returns. hero_lede_2: In collaboration with the **Traffic Safety Ad-Hoc** of the Los Gatos Youth Commission, LG Think Tank ran a federally prescribed cost-benefit analysis to determine the real impact of Route 27. Even the **current** network, flawed as it is, already returns **$1.71 in benefits for every $1 spent** over 20 years. This feedback loop - the **public transit death spiral** - ends with the empty stops already visible around town. The **key to reversing** the Los Gatos transit death spiral **lies in our students**. Adults have convenient transit alternatives and must often travel outside of town for work. But students appear at **predictable times** and **predictable places** in accordance with **school schedules** — a boon for underfunded bus routes looking for the **easy ridership boost** that can justify increased investment in an underserved area. Many residents need public transit. Students, for example, gain **true independence** - the ability to attend school, meet up with friends, or go to the movies without finding a ride from parents. Meanwhile, the socioeconomically disadvantaged can afford to live and work in our beautiful town without the exorbitant cost of personal transit. The federal government has already done the hard work of figuring out how to value transit honestly. All the benefits of public transit are quantified under [FTA cost-benefit guidelines](analysis.html#glossary) and [USDOT BCA Guidance 2024](analysis.html#glossary). So we ran the numbers, for the existing network and our redesigned Route 27 with stops where the demand actually is. The result? An **exhaustively empirical** picture of the next steps for public transit in Los Gatos, a roadmap for a more vibrant and welcoming community. - heading: What this analysis is and is not body: > This is a **public-interest analysis**, not a paid advocacy document.
